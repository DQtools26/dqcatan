Events

Objective: Reach 10 VP. You cannot win with unpaid debt, during wait penalties, or while restricted by active economic events.

Turn structure: Roll → Resource Distribution → Actions (optional) → Event Resolution → Summary → Pass device.

Double Roll: If the same total is rolled twice in a row, Bank Collapse triggers.

Bankruptcy / Reset: If a player cannot repay after forced property sales, reset to minimal state (1 road, 1 settlement).

Global timers: Event durations are global turns, not per-player turns.

Deposits: Locked in bank and cannot be spent until withdrawn. Deposit opportunities are tracked by the banker; one deposit per resource type per opportunity.

Debit Card: Issued by banker. Direct withdrawals from savings/deposits. Fee: 1 resource every 10 turns.

Withdraw (Debit): Move deposited resources to hand. Direct, costs nothing extra (besides the maintenance fee).

Full Withdrawal: Take all deposits at once. Cost: 1 of each resource type currently deposited. Must wait 3 turns to reopen account.

Standard Loan: 20% interest (rounded up), due in 5 turns. Repay principal in same resource, interest in any resource.

Rate increases +5% per previous late loan.

Late (overdue): +25% penalty fee. Cannot buy, trade, or win. After repayment: 3-turn wait before winning again.

Cannot take a new loan if you have overdue loans.

Opening: Pay 3 of any one resource to bank.

Maintenance: 1 resource every 5 turns.

Borrowing: 10% interest (+10% per late fee), due in 2 turns. Same late rules as standard loan (3-turn wait, cannot win/buy/trade).

Building perk: Ignore 1 resource cost per build action.

Pay in any resource except the one ordered.

Fees: 1–2 units: +1 | 3–6 units: +3 | 7+: +4

Standard delivery: 4 turns. Expedited: 2 turns (double fee).

Suspended during economic downturns.

Any two players can create a rental agreement. The tenant pays the landlord periodically.

Settlement: 1 Wool + 1 Brick every 2 turns

City: 1 Wood + 1 Wool + 1 Brick every 2 turns

Road Toll: 2 Wood every 3 turns

Suspended during economic downturns.

Each resource has a stock price (in resource units). Buy shares using any resource. Sell to receive that resource at current price.

Prices fluctuate each turn based on dice rolls.

Cannot buy stocks with overdue loans.

Economic Crisis: Rich players may be sabotaged each turn.

Economic Boom: Production bonuses.

Recession (4 turns): No banking, shipping, rentals, or winning.

Depression (7 turns): Same as recession but longer and more severe.
