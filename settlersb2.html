Goal

Be the first to reach 10 Victory Points on your own turn, with no unpaid debt and no active downturn.

Turn Order — Every Turn

Step 1 — Roll Dice. Roll both dice. Resources are produced. Rolling the exact same total as the previous turn triggers a Bank Collapse.

Step 2 — Distribute Resources. Every settlement and city on a hex matching the roll produces. Update each player's hand in the software. Banked (savings) resources are NOT affected by the robber on a 7 — only hand resources are at risk.

Step 3 — Actions (all optional). In any order, you may: Build, Trade with other players, use the Bank, place Shipping orders, manage Rentals, Gamble, or buy/sell Stocks. You are never required to do any of these.

Step 4 — Events. Software checks for overdue loans, upcoming payments, and active events. Resolve anything flagged.

Step 5 — Summary. Review your VP, resources, and loans. If you have 10+ VP and no restrictions, declare victory now. Otherwise pass the device.

Build Costs

🛣️ Road: 1 Wood + 1 Brick

🏠 Settlement: 1 Wood + 1 Brick + 1 Wool + 1 Grain

🏙️ City (upgrade a settlement): 3 Ore + 2 Grain

🃏 Dev Card: 1 Ore + 1 Wool + 1 Grain

Rankings (used for taxes & events)

Rankings are based on total assets — Savings + Stock value + Property value. Hand resources do NOT count.

Property value: Settlement = 1 unit, City = 2 units, Road = 0 units.

Rich = top 2 players by assets. Poor = lowest player. Everyone else = Middle.

How to Win

You must have 10 or more VP at the start of your turn to declare victory. You cannot win at the end of someone else's turn.

VP Sources

🏠 Each Settlement = 1 VP

🏙️ Each City = 2 VP

🛣️ Longest Road (5+ roads, must be longest) = +2 VP

⚔️ Largest Army (3+ knights, must be largest) = +2 VP

🃏 Victory Point Dev Cards = +1 VP each

Win Blockers — You CANNOT win if any of these apply

❌ You have any overdue (unpaid past due date) loan of any type.

❌ You are in a wait period — a 3-turn restriction imposed after paying off an overdue loan.

❌ An economic downturn (Recession or Depression) is currently active. Nobody wins during a downturn.

The software enforces all of the above automatically. If it shows WIN! on your card, you are clear. If not, the reason is shown in your summary.

Bankruptcy

If you cannot pay a debt (loan interest, rent, tax, or maintenance fee), you must sell assets to the bank at half price until the debt is covered:

Settlement → 2 resources (grain) | City → 3 resources | Road → 1 resource

If you have no assets left, the software resets you to 1 settlement + 1 road + 3-turn wait. You are still in the game.

Savings Account

Cost to open: 2 resources (any type, paid to the bank).

Locked storage. Resources in savings cannot be spent, traded, or used for building until withdrawn. They are completely off limits.

Safe from the robber. When a 7 is rolled, the robber only steals from hand resources. Savings are untouchable.

Growth: Every 3 of your own turns (not global turns), you earn +1 resource per 3 resources stored, rounded down. Example: 7 stored → +2 resources added.

7-roll bonus: When any player rolls a 7, you immediately receive +1 resource for each different type of resource you have in savings. Example: you have Wool + Ore + Grain saved → +3 resources on a 7.

Debit Card

Cost to open: 2 resources (paid to the bank). Issued by the Banker in the software.

Maintenance: 1 resource every 3 turns. Paid automatically; if you can't pay, the banker is notified.

Benefits: Allows direct withdrawal from savings at any time. Also lets you ignore 1 resource cost when building (same as Credit Card perk).

Withdrawals

Debit Withdrawal: Requires active debit card. Move any amount from savings to hand instantly. No extra cost beyond the card maintenance.

Full Withdrawal: Take everything out at once. Cost: 1 resource of each type currently saved (not total amount — one per type). After a full withdrawal, you must wait 3 turns before reopening your savings account.

Example: You have 4 Wool, 2 Ore, 3 Grain saved. Full withdrawal costs 1 Wool + 1 Ore + 1 Grain from your hand, and you receive all 9 saved resources.

Standard Loan

Borrow any amount of a single resource type from the bank.

Interest: 20%, rounded up. Example: borrow 3 Ore → owe 3 Ore + 1 any resource in interest.

Due: 5 turns from when you took the loan.

Repayment: Principal must be paid back in the same resource you borrowed. Interest can be paid in any resource.

Rate penalty: Each time you miss a loan deadline in the past, your interest rate goes up by +5%. A player who has been late twice pays 30% instead of 20%.

Late / Overdue

If you do not repay by the due turn, the loan becomes OVERDUE.

Penalty: +25% of the principal added to what you owe.

While overdue, you cannot: build, trade, buy stocks, take new loans, or win the game.

After you finally repay an overdue loan, you must wait 3 turns before you can win again.

You also cannot take a new loan while you have any overdue loan.

Credit Card

Cost to open: Pay 3 of the same resource to the bank.

Maintenance: 1 resource every 5 turns, paid automatically.

Building perk: When building, if you are 1 resource short, your credit card automatically covers it — no need to borrow separately. This creates a Credit Loan.

Borrowing directly: You can also borrow resources through the card at any time during your turn.

Credit Loans

Interest: 10% per loan. Each time you've been late in the past, future CC loans cost +10% more.

Due: 2 turns (fast — plan ahead).

Late penalty: +20% of the amount added to what you owe. Same win/trade/build block as standard loans. 3-turn wait after repayment.

Example: You borrow 2 Brick via CC. Interest = ceil(2 × 0.10) = 1 any resource. Total to repay: 2 Brick + 1 any.

How Stocks Work

Each resource (Wool, Wood, Brick, Ore, Grain) has a stock price shown in the market ticker. You buy shares using any resources you have, and sell shares to receive that resource back at the current price.

Buying: Pay resources totaling (price × shares), rounded up. You can use any mix of resources.

Selling: Receive (price × shares) of that specific resource, rounded down.

Cannot buy if you have any overdue loan.

What Controls Stock Prices

Prices recalculate automatically after every dice roll based on four real factors. The Stocks tab shows exactly why each price moved.

1. Market Scarcity (biggest factor)

The software counts how much of each resource players are holding in their hands. Low average supply = high demand = price goes UP. High supply = price goes DOWN.

Average hand <1.5 per player: +0.40 | 1.5–3: +0.15 | 3–6: −0.10 | 6+: −0.35

2. Hoarding / Savings

Resources locked in savings accounts are off the open market. Every unit banked adds +0.05 upward pressure (max +0.30). Hoarders make scarce resources more expensive for everyone.

3. Dice Roll

Rolling a 7 causes market disruption — the robber shakes confidence, causing a random swing of ±0.30 on all resources. High-production numbers (6 and 8) slightly lower prices (−0.10) because those hexes produce often. Other numbers add small random noise.

4. Active Economic Events

Boom: +0.25 all stocks | Crisis: −0.20 all stocks | Recession: −0.20 | Depression: −0.40

Strategy tip: Buy when a resource is plentiful in everyone's hands (low price). Then hoard it in savings to push the price back up before selling.

Shipping (Global Trade)

Order any resource from the global market. You cannot pay the fee using the resource you are ordering — use anything else.

Fees & Delivery

1–2 units ordered: +1 resource fee

3–6 units ordered: +3 resource fee

7+ units ordered: +4 resource fee

Standard delivery: 3 turns.

Expedited delivery: Arrives next turn. Fee is doubled.

Example: Order 4 Ore (standard). Fee = 3. Pay 3 of any non-Ore resources. Ore arrives in 3 turns.

Shipping is fully suspended during Recession and Depression. Orders already in transit are cancelled. No refunds on fees already paid.

Rental Agreements

Any two players can create a rental. The landlord owns the property; the tenant pays periodically. The tenant must actually have the resources to pay — if they can't, the banker is notified and the landlord can choose to end the agreement or wait.

Rental Rates

🏠 Settlement: 1 Wool + 1 Brick, due every 2 turns.

🏙️ City: 1 Wood + 1 Wool + 1 Brick, due every 2 turns.

🛣️ Road Toll: 2 Wood, due every 3 turns. Multiple tenants can have toll agreements on the same road — each pays separately.

Rules

The landlord still owns the property for VP purposes. Renting does not transfer ownership.

Either player can end the agreement on their turn — but rent already due must still be paid first.

Rentals are suspended during downturns. No payments are collected or owed during Recession or Depression turns.

Bank Collapse

Trigger: Rolling the same total as the previous roll (double roll).

Effects:

• Everyone loses 50% of their hand resources (rounded up — worse for the rich).

• All stock prices drop 50%.

• All active loans become immediately due.

• All savings deposits are wiped.

• All shipping orders and rentals are cancelled.

Downturn determination: Each player must sell 1 property OR pay 3 resources. Count how many players sell property. 2+ sellers → Depression (7 turns). 0–1 sellers → Recession (4 turns).

⚠ Mandatory Bank Manager Ritual

The Bank Manager must physically fall to the floor, cry dramatically, and lament the collapse out loud. Failure to perform the ritual = the game ends with no winner. This is not optional.

Recession (4 global turns) & Depression (7 global turns)

During a downturn, nobody can: take loans, use savings, use credit cards, ship orders, collect rent, or win the game.

Building and trading between players are still allowed.

Duration counts in full rounds (everyone takes one turn = 1 global turn).

Economic Boom (3 global turns)

Build costs reduced by 1 resource (minimum 1). All stock prices trend upward (+0.25/turn). Triggered by Banker.

Economic Crisis (3 global turns)

Each turn, Rich players must pay 2 resources to the bank OR lose all of one resource type from their hand (their choice). Stock prices trend downward (−0.20/turn). Triggered by Banker.

Tax Collection

Taxes are collected every 5 global turns (every full round of all players, 5 times over). The Banker triggers collection.

Tax Rates by Rank

Ranking is based on total assets (savings + stock value + property value — NOT hand resources).

🟡 Rich (top 2 players): Pay 2 resources per property unit. Settlement = 1 unit, City = 2 units.

⚪ Middle (everyone else): Pay 1 resource per property unit.

🔴 Poor (lowest asset player): Exempt. Pay nothing.

Payment Order

Pay from hand first. If hand is insufficient, sell assets at half price. If still can't pay, forced liquidation (same as bankruptcy).

Tax Evasion

Instead of paying, you may attempt tax evasion. Roll 1 die:

🎲 1–3: Caught! Pay double the original tax amount.

🎲 4–6: Escaped! Pay nothing this tax cycle.

High risk, high reward. Only available once per tax cycle per player.

⚖ Official Dispute Rulings

The software is the final authority. If the software allows or blocks an action, that ruling stands. These rulings cover common arguments.

"I should be able to win, I have 10 VP!"

❌ Denied if you have any overdue loan, are in a wait period, or a downturn is active. VP alone is not enough. All three conditions must be clear simultaneously on your turn.

"I paid my loan, the wait period is unfair."

✅ The 3-turn wait is correct. Paying off an overdue loan does not immediately restore your win eligibility. The wait is the penalty for being late — not for paying. It counts down from the moment of repayment.

"My savings should count as resources."

❌ No. Savings are locked. You cannot use them for building, trading, paying loans, or rent until you withdraw them. They only count toward your asset ranking for tax purposes.

"The robber should steal my banked resources."

❌ No. Savings accounts are protected. Only hand resources can be discarded or targeted by the robber. This is a core mechanic — savings are insurance.

"We didn't actually roll a double — it's a coincidence."

✅ A double roll is a double roll. The previous turn's total is shown in the navbar. If the new roll matches it exactly, Bank Collapse triggers. Intent doesn't matter.

"The Bank Manager refuses to do the collapse ritual."

🔴 The game ends immediately with no winner. This is not a joke rule. The ritual is mandatory. Players should vote on whether the performance was sincere.

"I want to trade during a downturn."

✅ Allowed. Player-to-player trading is still permitted during Recession and Depression. What's suspended: banking, loans, savings, credit cards, shipping, and rentals.

"Stock prices are unfair / rigged."

✅ Prices are driven by actual game state. Check the Stocks tab — it shows exactly why each price moved (scarcity, hoarding, dice, events). If prices feel unfair, that's the market. Adapt.

"Can I repay a loan early?"

✅ Yes, any time during your turn. Early repayment has no penalty. Full interest still applies — you owe the same interest whether you pay on turn 1 or turn 5.

"Can I take out a loan to pay off another loan?"

✅ Yes, if neither loan is overdue. You cannot take a new loan if you already have an overdue one. But borrowing to service a non-overdue loan is a valid (if risky) strategy.

"The rent is too high / I didn't agree to that."

✅ Rental rates are fixed by the rules (shown in the Rentals tab). Both players must agree to create the agreement — but once created, the rate is fixed. Either player can end it on their turn, but current due payments must be settled first.
