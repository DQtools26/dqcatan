ECONOMIC EVENTS

The Market
Does Not Care.

Economic events fire automatically — triggered by dice rolls, player actions, and pure misfortune. No one is safe. Plan accordingly.

RECESSION
4 Global Turns
Loans, savings, credit, shipping, and rentals all suspended. You can still build and trade. Victory is blocked.
DEPRESSION
7 Global Turns — 3 Phases
Phase 1: Lose 50% hand, stocks −50%, loans due. Phase 2: Market stagnation. Phase 3: Slow recovery. VP blocked throughout.
BOOM
3 Global Turns
Build costs −1 resource (min 1). Stocks +0.25 per turn. May occur after a Depression ends. Enjoy it while it lasts.
CRISIS
3 Global Turns
Rich players must pay 2 resources to the bank or lose all of one resource type. Stocks −0.20 per turn.
BANK COLLAPSE
Instant
Roll the same total twice in a row. Lose 50% hand. Stocks −50%. All loans due immediately. Savings wiped. And someone hits the floor.

OFFICIAL RULEBOOK

Everything You
Need to Know.

Settlers: Capitalism runs on the classic Catan board. If you've never played Catan, grab the base rules below. Then layer in the Capitalism rules on top.

Settlers: Capitalism is played on a standard Catan board. You need to know the basics before layering in the economic rules.

The Board

19 terrain hexes arranged in a honeycomb, surrounded by ocean. Each hex produces a resource when its number is rolled. Settlements and cities sit at hex intersections (corners), and roads run along hex edges.

Resources

🐑 Wool — Pasture
🪵 Wood — Forest
🧱 Brick — Hills
⛏️ Ore — Mountains
🌾 Grain — Fields

Your Turn (Base)

  • Roll both dice. All players receive resources from hexes matching the roll if they have a settlement or city there.
  • Trade. Negotiate with other players, or trade 4:1 with the bank (or 3:1 / 2:1 at harbors).
  • Build. Spend resources to build roads, settlements, cities, or buy development cards.

Rolling a 7

No resources are produced. Anyone with more than 7 cards discards half (round down). You move the Robber to any hex, blocking its production, and steal 1 card from an adjacent player.

Development Cards

  • Knight: Move the robber. Collect 3 to claim Largest Army (+2 VP).
  • Progress: Road Building (2 free roads), Year of Plenty (take any 2 resources), Monopoly (take all of one resource type from all players).
  • Victory Point: Hidden until you win. Reveal only when declaring victory.
You cannot win on another player's turn. Victory is declared at the start of your turn if you have 10+ VP.

First to 10 Victory Points on their own turn wins — but there are blockers.

VP Sources

SourceVP
🏠 Settlement1 VP

🛣️ Longest Road (5+ roads, must be longest)+2 VP

Win Blockers

  • Any overdue loan (standard or credit)
  • 3-turn wait after paying off an overdue loan
  • Active Recession or Depression
Even if you have 10 VP, you cannot declare victory while any of these blockers are active.

Step 1 — Roll Dice

Roll both dice. Resources are produced by matching hexes. Rolling the exact same total as the previous turn triggers a Bank Collapse.

Step 2 — Resource Distribution

The software updates all hand and banked resources. Banked (savings) resources are NOT affected by the robber on a 7 — only hand resources are at risk of theft or event penalties.

Step 3 — Actions (All Optional, Any Order)

  • Build (settlements, roads, cities, dev cards)
  • Trade with other players
  • Banking (savings, loans, cards)
  • Place shipping orders
  • Manage rentals
  • Gamble
  • Buy or sell stocks

Step 4 — Event Resolution

Software checks for overdue loans, active economic events, and Bank Collapse triggers.

Step 5 — Summary

Review VP, hand, banked resources, loans, and rankings. Pass the device.

Card Perks

  • Debit Card: Can ignore 1 resource cost when building.
  • Credit Card: Automatically covers 1 missing resource — but creates a Credit Loan.

During a Boom, all build costs are reduced by 1 resource (minimum 1).

Rankings are based on assets only — Savings + Stock value + Property value. Hand resources do NOT count.

Property values: Settlement = 1 unit, City = 2 units, Road = 0 units.

Rank Tiers

  • Rich = top 2 players by assets
  • Middle = everyone else
  • Poor = lowest player (exempt from taxes)

Tax (Every 5 Global Turns)

RankTax Rate
Rich2 resources per property unit
Middle1 resource per property unit
PoorExempt

Payment order: Hand resources → Sell resources → Sell property (half price if forced).

Tax Evasion (Optional, Once Per Cycle)

Roll 1 die. 1–3: Caught — pay double tax. 4–6: Escaped — pay nothing.

Savings Account

  • Cost to open: 2 resources
  • Cannot be stolen or used for building/trading until withdrawn
  • Growth: +1 per 3 resources banked, every 3 turns
  • 7-roll bonus: +1 per resource type stored

Debit Card

  • Cost: 2 resources
  • Maintenance: 1 resource every 3 turns
  • Allows direct withdrawal from savings + 1 resource discount on builds

Credit Card

  • Cost: 3 resources
  • Maintenance: 1 resource every 5 turns
  • Automatically covers 1 missing resource when building → creates a Credit Loan

Credit Loan

  • Interest: 10%
  • Due in: 2 turns
  • Late penalty: +20% per turn overdue
  • Blocks winning, trading, and building while overdue
  • 3-turn wait after repayment before winning

Standard Loan

  • Borrow any amount of a single resource type
  • Interest: 20% (+5% per previous late repayment on record)
  • Due in: 5 turns
  • Late penalty: +25% per turn overdue
  • Cannot take a new loan while any loan is overdue
Loan math gets expensive fast. The software calculates it — you just have to live with the consequences.

You can rent out your settlements, cities, or roads to other players for passive resource income.

  • Settlements & Cities: Tenant pays every turn.
  • Roads: Tenant pays every 3 turns.
  • The landlord always retains VP ownership of the property.
  • Rentals are suspended during Recession and Depression.
Build property, collect rent, and let someone else worry about resources. The landlord life is real, even in Catan.

Bank Collapse

Trigger: Rolling the same total as the previous turn.

  • Lose 50% hand resources (round up; wealthier players are hit harder)
  • All stocks −50%
  • All loans immediately due
  • All savings wiped
  • All shipping and rentals canceled
  • 0–1 sellers during collapse → Recession (4 turns)
  • 2+ sellers → Depression (7 turns)
  • Bank Manager must perform the ritual. No exceptions.

Recession (4 Global Turns)

Suspends: loans, savings, credit, shipping, rentals, and winning. Trading and building remain allowed.

Depression — Three-Phase (7 Global Turns)

  • Turns 1–2 (Immediate Shock): Lose 50% hand, stocks −50%, loans due, shipping/rentals frozen.
  • Turns 3–5 (Market Stagnation): No loans, savings, or stock purchases. Trading allowed.
  • Turns 6–7 (Recovery): Savings access restored, credit card allowed, shipping resumes. VP collection still blocked.
  • A Boom may occur after Phase 3 ends.

Boom (3 Global Turns)

  • Build costs −1 resource (min 1)
  • Stocks +0.25 per turn

Crisis (3 Global Turns)

  • Rich players: pay 2 resources to bank, or lose all of one resource type
  • Stocks −0.20 per turn
